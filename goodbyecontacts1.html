GoodbyeContacts Mobile app

UX-UI - App Redesign

Links App Demo

Role UX UI Designer

Duration March 2017 - May 2017

Design the overall look and feel of GoodByeContacts. A fun IOS App that allows people to delete contacts and have a fun message appear or even an emoji.  My process includes research, sketches, and wireframes.

Tools Sketch, Axure, InVision

Design Process

I reviewed some ideas from the product owner and stayed with an open mind. At first I had no clue what this engineer really likes or wants for his App redesign. I approached the situation with an open mind and followed up with solutions to pick the product owners brain.

We talked back and forth about what he wanted in the redesign for his app.

Research

Through remote calls and chat

 

I asked the product owner who is an IOS developer to iterate on what his app is and what it does. I asked him various questions to nail down the details for this redesign.

Moodboard

Gather inspiration and share with client

 

I Created a moodboard for ideas generation. After speaking with the product owner he gave me some ideas to approach the re-design. First and foremost, I created a collage of different Apps, websites, and colors to get an idea of where to head first. I even placed different fonts to get a gist of what works for him.

Redesign at a Glance

In order to optimize my workflow for this redesign, I evaluated my approach.

keep the original developed idea of of swiping left to delete a contact, or swiping right to keep the contact. Have a present design trend so it doesn't look obsolete. Let the user feel empathy when swiping left or right and keep in mind affordance. This would bring funny or sad quotes and emoji's.

Ideation: Low-Fi Sketches - Three Ideas

The challenge was to keep his original app structure. By keeping the product owner in mind I quickly sketched out 3 versions of the app and presented them to him.

Feedback From the Stakeholder

The product owner really liked the middle sketch buttons due to its location and roundness.  However I wasn't sure he could add the contacts name within the image that is brought to attention. He is able to code this in after speaking with him. He  liked the first sketch and added that I can substitute the bottom button with the sketch from the middle. The third sketch (Right) the design is more streamlined as it follows the larger text within the middle of screen. I also had to combat other areas of technical constraints.

"With the library I'm using at the moment I'm not 100% sure if I can show the next contact in the list but I can always omit that from the design."

Final Sketch & Flow

Reworking the design I created a better solution to combat the contacts that was initially omitted. Later he wanted to add where you can see the contacts in the list before swiping.

 

Knowing what the product is and how users will interact with the interface is important. I took the original structure form and assessed the current flow into a newer structured app design. I finalized the design will low-fi sketches and presented to the product owner.

 

Taking the sketches I created a new flow to help understand how this new structure will flow for the projected users.

Some of the updated emoji's: when users swipe left or right.

Final Design

Usablity Testing - (A/B Testing)

Measured: Satisfaction, ease of use, emoji likeness

Changed the overall color scheme from all white to a dash of color of blue, green and red. It was hard to differentiate the all blue text for start over and Delete. I changed the text color to better suit the display.

This allows the user to get a better gist of the branding of the app itself. The product owner really didn't have such a brand but had ideas for just an app instead. By adding the color choice It really helped gear him forward to making the branding for the app.

Final Design Ideation

The design is straight forward and far updated versus the original App. However the product owner wanted a touch of color. I updated the color with some red for the delete button and blue top navigation bar.
