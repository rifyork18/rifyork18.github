About

Archi-AR

Role UX Designer

UX Design - Interactive Prototyping

Duration Oct 2018 - Nov 2018

Is a mobile augmented reality app that allows users to visually build and observe floor plan drafts of residential and commercial plans in a 3D visualized presentation. The app also allows for easy documentation and sharing with the team or your clients.

Tools Sketch, XD, InVision, Spark AR Studio Cardboard cut out

Overview

Problem

 

How might we improve floor plan draft discrepancy through intuitive augmented reality.

Solution

 

I will create an intuitive way for architects to generate 3D models based off defined floor plans and a way to document the content.

Process

Problem Existence

The process of analyzing 3D visualization from a floorplan can be time consuming and costly. In my previous experience I have worked with designers, architects, and the like and I can relate to the process. I want to create a quicker way of translating 3D while using new technology.

Research

Literature Review

By understanding 3D technology and augmented reality we can better grasp how the two entities coincide together. Using an image or image target via AR serves as a tracking base for the augmented reality feature to work in the real world space. I studied further and gathered related information about how video games use such a feature.

Affinity Mapping

I created an affinity map to help me dial down the insights from the interviews and contextual inquiry (field study). I realized the users have an easy time using 3D software to produce results but it can be time consuming.

Task Flow

 I used a task flowchart for the data to present the information through a specific task.

Competitive & Comparative Analysis

I did a competitive analysis to get an understanding of how other products are doing in the market and what features they use. This helped me structure the app accordingly from in depth qualitative aspects.

Personas

I create personas or hypothetical users that represent my target users. These personas are  based off the initial user interviews. This also helps me outline my goals and strategies.

Storyboards

A storyboard helps visually navigate what the users problem is and how can we solve it.

Design Ideation - App Flow

While keeping the users in mind, I sketched out different iterations of the app. From the personas I created based off actual target users I kept in mind their needs and goals. I then created something that will work. I outlined a flow and annotated the process.

The initial user created a project from the home page, creates a plan which allows for various floorplans to be within the project. Users adds options within the list. Then user takes picture of floorplan to create an image target. Then the user use the AR feature. Lastly the user can save the 3D model and analyze it.

Lo-FI Wireframe Prototyping

Cardboard + AR Prototyping

I decided to create a cardboard prototype to better help participants realize the augmented reality feature and 3D Visualization. I used a  cardboard cut out shaped into a iPhone and a foam board cutout for the 3D visualization.

HI-FI Mockups

From our personas needs, I kept the design easy to read, organized and not very busy. I added an easy and organized way of project management and note taking to help the users quickly work with their clients needs.

Building - 3D Assets

In order to recreate the visualization you need an image target for the Augmented reality feature to track in the real world, hence the real world floor plan. I placed the floor plan into a 3D software such as Autodesk Maya and used this as my image target. I modeled the floor plan in 3D and exported the model  into Spark AR studio to prototype the Augmented reality feature.

Testing this feature helped users grasp the idea of a augmented reality feature within the app.

Usability Testing - Reiterating

The participants had a hard time understanding the buttons on the render screen, so I labeled them accordingly to reduce confusion of some of the  features.
